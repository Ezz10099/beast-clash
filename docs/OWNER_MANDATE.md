# Pixel Mage — Owner Mandate

## Purpose

This file preserves the owner's correction about how Pixel Mage must be developed. It exists so future sessions do not keep reopening the project, replacing the core game casually, or treating fun as a secondary concern behind scope, process, or easy validation.

## Binding Development Mandate

1. **Keep one project.** Pixel Mage in `Ezz10099/beast-clash` remains the active game. Do not propose switching projects or replacing the core direction unless strong evidence shows that the current game cannot satisfy the Product Compass, or the owner explicitly asks to reconsider it.
2. **Finish a worthwhile game, not merely a finished build.** The goal is still to complete and publish a realistic Android game through the GitHub–SPCK–Capacitor workflow. Finishability is a constraint that protects completion; it must not become an excuse to release a technically complete but weak, shallow, or forgettable game.
3. **Fun and engagement are primary design priorities.** Every material decision must actively improve or protect moment-to-moment enjoyment, meaningful choices, escalation, build excitement, replay desire, curiosity, mastery, surprise, and satisfying payoff.
4. **Do not defer creative responsibility to testers.** Human testing is necessary for subjective confirmation, but lack of immediate testers does not justify stopping product thinking. Codex must independently use the full development model: player-journey inspection, alternatives, public-human evidence, comparable games, component and experience analogues, original synthesis, bounded prototypes, synthetic play, fresh-context criticism, and prediction calibration.
5. **Evidence serves fun; it does not replace it.** Automated checks, scope control, documentation, and release safety are supporting systems. They must prevent false confidence and wasted work, but they must not dominate the product so strongly that fun and engagement receive less attention.
6. **Do not confuse unfamiliarity with invalid testing.** Arabic-speaking players or players unfamiliar with genre terms can still provide valuable evidence. Prefer visual clarity, simple language, Arabic localization where needed, and observation of behavior instead of assuming that English vocabulary is required to test the game.
7. **Use the smallest scope that is genuinely worthwhile.** Avoid both endless expansion and premature minimalism. The target is the strongest fun and engagement potential that can still be completed, polished, packaged, tested, and published.
8. **Do not wait for the owner to invent the game.** The owner should not have to repeatedly diagnose missing fun, generate every mechanic, or remind Codex of the final goal. Codex owns proactive product development and must surface the strongest next improvement itself.

## Required Session Interpretation

At the beginning of every meaningful session, interpret the project this way:

- The game is Pixel Mage; continuity is the default.
- The final Product Compass remains binding.
- Core mechanics are revisable only when that improves the same game, not as casual project switching.
- The first product question is: **what most limits fun, engagement, build excitement, or replay desire now?**
- The second question is: **what is the strongest finishable improvement that addresses it?**
- Scope, evidence, architecture, and testing must support those questions.
